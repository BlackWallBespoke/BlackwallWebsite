Synthetic Handle Materials

Engineered to Endure

Synthetic handle materials offer properties that wood cannot replicate — extreme resistance to moisture, temperature swings, and wear; visual effects impossible in natural grain; colours that do not fade; surfaces that do not absorb what they encounter. Each synthetic handle is turned by hand on the lathe to the same exacting standard as the wood line. The finished piece is indistinguishable in quality from its wooden counterpart, and often surpasses it in durability. For certain applications and aesthetics, there is no substitute.

Fordite — If Available (Market pricing)

Detroit Agate.
A literal piece of industrial history.
Layers of automotive enamel paint, built up over decades on the jigs and skids of old car assembly lines, then cut and polished like stone.
Every piece is a geological record of production — the colour bands are the actual paint schemes of actual cars, compressed into a material that no longer exists anywhere else.
Historical Fordite cannot be manufactured: It can only be found.
When a blank presents itself that is suitable for turning, it is offered.
There is no other way to acquire it.

Historical Fordite is a finite material. The assembly lines that produced it no longer exist, and the supply diminishes with every piece cut.

Pricing reflects both the rarity and the individual character of each piece, and is confirmed at the time the blank is presented.

If historical Fordite is of interest, mention it during the commission conversation — pieces are occasionally held for clients with a standing interest.

Recreated Fordite — From $5,000

A commission-specific process in which the layered enamel structure of historical Fordite is deliberately recreated from scratch. Automotive-grade enamels are applied in successive coats, built up to the required depth, cured between layers, and then turned on the lathe as a single blank.

The palette, layer sequence, and thickness distribution are designed in collaboration with the client. The result is a piece with the same cross-sectional character as the original — banded, geological, unrepeatable — but conceived entirely for the commission rather than found.

This process is extremely long, incredibly expensive, and highly labour intensive labour intensive.
Shattered pieces that require a complete restart of the creation are extremely common, even for the most experienced turners. While every Blackwall piece has no concrete date of completion, Recreated Fordite buyers should expect significant wait times beyond anything you would experience from even the rarest wood handles: Recreating decades of history is neither quick nor easy.

The result of this patient, time-consuming process is an absolutely heart-stopping piece, a museum-quality heirloom of singular beauty, unmatched by anything other than histotical Fordite.

The starting price reflects the time, materials, and irreversibility of the process. Each recreated Fordite handle is a one-of-one object by definition.
