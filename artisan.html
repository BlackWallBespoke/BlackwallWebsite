The Hand Behind the Work

J. Christophe
Aishe

Artisan - Maker - Craftsman

The hand behind the work is J. Christophe Aishe, master turner with more than three decades of experience.

For over twenty years, J. Christophe Aishe built people.

As a high-performance rehabilitation specialist, he was an architect of human potential. He understood the intricate map of muscles, the resilience of tendons, and the precise biomechanical balance of the body in motion. Hands-on work dedicated to helping athletes recover from injury and rebuild themselves stronger than before, teaching them that true strength lies not in brute force, but in control, precision, and mindful movement.

But life has a way of shifting the foundation.

Disability ended his career in the performance lab, but it couldn't end his need to create. Instead, it simply redirected the tools in his hands.

That redirect led him back to the lathe, a place of memory and smell and dust. J. Christophe learned to turn wood as a boy, standing at the elbow of his grandfather in his workshop.

As he puts it, "Spending $1200 on a flogger is perhaps no longer the domain of the rich seeking pieces of status, but rather also the connoisseur of Kink, and the serious practitioner of the craft.
