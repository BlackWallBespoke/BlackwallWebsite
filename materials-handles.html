Handle Materials

Wood

Hand-turned on a lathe, one at a time. From common domestic species to the rarest of exotic woods available only when the right blank presents itself. Each wood species has its own weight, grain character, and working personality — all of which affect the finished piece.

Domestic — Included in Base Price

Ash
Pale, tight grain. Exceptional strength-to-weight ratio.
Beech
Fine, even texture. Finishes beautifully on a lathe.
Birch
Creamy white to pale brown. Smooth and consistent grain.
Cedar — Aromatic
Distinctive warm scent. Reddish heartwood. Very light weight.
Cherry
Rich amber-red. Darkens beautifully with age and use.
Maple — Ambrosia
Creamy base with dark fungal streaks. Each piece is one-of-a-kind.
Maple — Roasted
Heat-treated for rich amber colour and stability.
Oak
Heavy, dense and durable with a pronounced open grain. A legendary domestic hardwood.
Hickory
Extremely hard and strong. The hardest wood native to North America.

Domestic Uncommon — +$25

Butternut
Lighter cousin of Walnut. Beautiful when cared for.
Walnut — Basic
The North American classic. The definition of refined taste.
Oak — White
Tighter grain than red oak. Perfect for a high-use piece.
Osage Orange
Will turn increasingly dark amber with UV exposure.
Cedar — Western Red
Aromatic. Reddish-brown, and extremely light.
Maple — Roasted Bird's Eye
Selected sections with distinct patterns. Extraordinary finish.

Special Domestic — $450–$600

Domestic species with exceptional figure, rarity, or character. Priced individually — confirmed during commission.

Maple — Quilted
Rippled, three-dimensional figure across the face. One of the most visually striking domestic woods available.
Holly
The whitest wood native to North America. Rarely available in turning blanks. Exceptional contrast against dark hardware.
Redwood
Warm, rich reddish-brown with a straight, even grain. Surprisingly light for its size.
Walnut — Black
Deeper, richer and more figured than basic walnut. A premium domestic with unmistakable character.

Non-Domestic — +$50

Mahogany — African
The gold standard for non-domestic mid-weight woods. Rich reddish-brown.
Tigerwood
Dense, strong and hard. Orange with dark parallel striping.
Olivewood
Creamy pale with irregular dark streaks. Grain can vary wildly.
Yellowheart
Vivid lemon-yellow. Striking when paired with dark falls and accessories.
Sapele
Starts dark brown, and darkens with age. Grain can differ wildly by piece.
Ipe
Extremely hard and dense, with a near-black finish.
