Wood-handle Flogger

Floggers with
Hand-Turned Wooden Handles

Every wooden handle begins as a raw blank- selected for grain, density, and figure. Each is meticulously turned by hand on the lathe to the exact diameter and profile specified in the order blank. No two handles are alike, because no two blanks are alike. The wood does not merely hold the falls; it is the primary aesthetic and tactile statement of the piece. Base pricing covers the full commission in standard configuration. Material upgrades, enhancements, and add-ons are priced separately.

Synthetic-handle Flogger

Floggers with
Hand-Turned Synthetic Handles

Synthetic handle materials offer properties unavailable in natural wood — extreme resistance to moisture, temperature, and wear, combined with visual characteristics that wood cannot replicate. Each synthetic handle is turned by hand on the lathe to the same exacting standard as the wood line. Material options include high-density acrylics, resin stabilised blanks, Corian, G10, and other engineered composites. Base pricing reflects standard configuration; material selection and enhancements are confirmed during the design conversation.

Metal-handled floggers

Floggers with
CNC Metal Handles

The metal handle line represents an entirely different order of object than blackwall's wood handles.

Each design is originated by Mr. Aishe, conceived as a three-dimensional form, computer modelled in full, and then machined to specification by CNC from a solid billet. The handle is not an extrusion or an assembly; it is a single piece of material, machined to tolerance and finished by hand. The design process is collaborative: the client's preferences inform the form, weight, surface treatment, and the integration of grip and neck elements into a unified whole.

Beespoke fitting can be done, although there are limitations depending on design and materials.

Metal handles are available in almost any machinable material. The standard offerings cover steel, bronze, copper, brass, aluminum, and carbon fibre, each with a distinct weight profile, surface quality, and visual language. Combinations, inlays, and mixed-material designs are sometimes possible, and can be discussed during the commission conversation.

The starting price reflects the additional design time, machining cost, and material quality inherent to a metal handle. Final pricing depends on material choice, design complexity, surface treatment, and fall specification. A metal handle commission requires a more detailed design conversation than a wooden handle commission- the form must be fully resolved before machining begins.

NOTE: Designing, perfecting and machining a cespoke, one-off metal handle is a laborious, time-consuming, and exceedingly expensive process. This is not an easy or quick endavour. Any company can churn out copies of a design a pay less for bulk volume, but making a one-of-one metal piece- especially one designed specifically for someone's hand- is not that process.
