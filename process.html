How It Works

No catalogues.
No stock. No shortcuts.

Every Blackwall piece begins with a conversation.

Not a form. Not a selection of pre-configured options.

 A genuine exchange between maker and client about what is being made, for whom, and why.

Step by Step

From first contact
to finished piece

01
The First Email

Reach out to info@blackwallbdsm.com with whatever you have in mind: A clear vision, a vague idea, or simply a desire to understand what is possible. There is no wrong starting point and no commitment required at this stage. Every piece begins with a question.

This is A conversation with the maker, not an automated reply. 
02
The Design Conversation

We discuss the piece in detail- type, handle, neck and butt hardware, fall species and cut, fall ends, any engraving or personalisation, and anything else that needs to be addressed. Every element is considered in relation to every other. Weight, balance, sensation, and aesthetics are all part of this conversation. It may take one exchange or several. It takes as long as it needs to.

No decisions are rushed    Revisions welcome before the order blank is signed
03
Biomechanical Analysis Optional

For clients who want a piece optimised specifically to their body and practice, a biomechanical analysis is available. This assesses hand size, grip strength, swing mechanics, and intended use pattern to determine the precise handle diameter, length, weight distribution, and fall configuration that will perform best for that individual. No two analyses produce the same result. This step adds meaningful depth to the piece, and meaningful time to the conversation.

Conducted by correspondence or in person where possible. 

Analysis Done via correspondence will require a kit being mailed to you for a cost 

The Results inform every dimension of the final spec

04
The Order Sheet & Deposit

When the design is settled, a detailed order sheet is completed. This sheet captures every specification of the piece in writing. This document becomes the contract between maker and client. A deposit is required at this stage to secure your place in the production queue. The deposit amount is confirmed during the design conversation and varies with the complexity and material value of the piece.

Your unique 5-digit order number is assigned at this stage      Deposit secures queue position
05
Material Sourcing

Wood blanks and leather hides are sourced and inspected by hand. For common domestic species and standard leathers, this is typically immediate. For rare exotics- African Blackwood, Gabon Ebony, Cocobolo, Kangaroo, Stingray- sourcing may require additional time. The right blank is always preferred over an expedient substitute. You will be informed of any material delay that affects the timeline before work proceeds.

Exotic material sourcing windows vary      Quality is never traded for speed
06
Handle Turning

The wood blank is mounted on the lathe, turned by hand, and assessed at every pass of the tool. The profile is developed in conversation with the grain of the specific piece of wood, not imposed on it. Diameter is held precisely to the spec established in the order blank. Where a biomechanical analysis has been conducted, grip shape may be subtly modified to suit the hand. Once the form is established, the handle is sanded through successive grits to 10,000, and finished with oil or wax appropriate to the species.

Progress photographed and posted to your private order page
More detailed progress tracking available for a fee
07
Leather Preparation & Fall Cutting

The selected hide is inspected once more- for consistency, for the absence of flaws at the cutting zone, and for thickness uniformity across the fall length. Falls are cut individually, not in batches, with attention to grain direction and edge quality. Width and length are held to the order blank specification. Ends- whether knotted, split, dragon-cut, braided, or woven- are executed after cutting. Where two-layer or clappered falls are specified, the layering and bonding is completed at this stage.

Fall count, width, length, and end treatment per order Sheet No shortcuts in cutting
08
Assembly & Wrapping

The handle, neck, falls, and grip are assembled into a single piece. Neck hardware is fitted and secured. Falls are gathered, ordered, and attached. Where a wrist tie is specified, it is measured, knotted, and threaded at this stage. The piece is weighed and balanced. Any asymmetry in fall hang is corrected before the assembly is finalised.

Balance and fall hang assessed at completion of assembly Corrections made before finishing
09
Finishing, Photography & Quality Review

The completed piece is inspected in full- handle, neck, grip, falls, butt, and every transition between them. Leather is conditioned. Hardware is polished or treated to the specified finish. Any engraving confirmed during the design conversation is executed at this stage on cooled, finished metal. The piece is then photographed- handle, falls, full length, and detail- and the images are posted to your private order page. A final photograph is also sent to you by email before the balance is requested.

Images posted to your private order page No balance requested until you have seen the completed piece
10
Payment, Packaging & Delivery

Once the balance is settled, the piece is prepared for shipping. Packaging is discreet- plain outer box, no exterior branding identifying the sender or contents beyond a return address. Internally, the piece is wrapped to protect the handle and prevent the falls from being crushed or set out of shape in transit. Tracking is provided. Shipping method and carrier are confirmed at this stage. Where a presentation case or leather roll pouch has been commissioned, the piece ships inside it.

Discreet packaging Full tracking provided Shipping costs confirmed at time of completion

Discretion

What is said between
us stays between us

Client names are never associated with order numbers online. Order pages are identified only by number. No photographs of your piece are published without your explicit permission. All communication is treated with strict confidentiality.

Shipping is in plain packaging. No exterior marking identifies the sender or contents beyond a return address. Your personal information is held offline only, and is never shared with third parties for any reason.

Questions

Frequently Asked

How long does a commission take? +
This depends significantly on material sourcing. Common domestic wood and standard leather â€” four to eight weeks from deposit to shipping. Exotic woods or rare hides can extend the timeline. The design conversation and queue position also affect timing. All timelines are discussed before the order blank is signed.
Can I change my order after it has been placed? +
Minor adjustments are sometimes possible in the early stages â€” before sourcing is complete and before any material has been cut. Changes after work has begun on the piece may not be possible, and in some cases may incur additional cost. This is discussed case by case.
Do you ship internationally? +
Yes. Shipping destinations are confirmed during the commission conversation. The client is responsible for understanding the import regulations of their country as they pertain to the materials in their piece. Some exotic materials have CITES restrictions that affect international shipping.
Can I see my piece before I pay the balance? +
Yes. A final photograph is posted to your private order page before the balance is requested. Additional detail photographs can be arranged by email.
Can I commission something not on the options list? +
The options list is a starting point, not a ceiling. If you have a specific vision that falls outside the listed options, bring it to the conversation. Not every request is achievable, but many are.
